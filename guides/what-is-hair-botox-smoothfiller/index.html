What Is 'Hair Botox'? Wella SmoothFiller, Explained

By the Hair Avenue Braddon team · Updated June 2026

First things first: no needles

"Hair botox" is a nickname, not an ingredient list. There's no botulinum toxin and nothing is injected — the name stuck because the treatment does for hair what cosmetic botox does for skin: fills, smooths and rejuvenates. At Hair Avenue we use Wella Professionals SmoothFiller, the salon-grade version of the concept.

What it actually does

SmoothFiller is a demi-permanent treatment whose Smart Delivery System carries active ingredients deep into the hair cortex, rebuilding internal bonds from the inside out. The visible result: dramatically less frizz, more shine, faster blow-dries and hair that behaves in humidity — without flattening your natural waves or curls. That last part matters: unlike straightening treatments, SmoothFiller preserves your texture.

Who it suits best

Anyone fighting frizz, flyaways or hair that won't hold a style — especially if you reach for the straightener daily. It's formaldehyde-free, safe for colour-treated hair, and results are cumulative: the more treatments you have, the smoother your hair becomes. If you want hair permanently straight rather than smoother, nanoplasty or Shiseido straightening is the better tool.

Cost, time and aftercare

At Hair Avenue Braddon, SmoothFiller starts from $249 and takes around 90 minutes to two hours. Results last up to three months. Aftercare is refreshingly boring: no waiting days to wash, no special shampoo rules, no harsh regrowth line — just smoother hair from the moment you leave the chair.

Quick answers

Is hair botox the same as keratin?

No. Keratin systems coat the hair and often straighten it; SmoothFiller rebuilds bonds inside the hair and keeps your natural texture.

How long does hair botox last?

Up to 3 months with Wella SmoothFiller, and results build with repeat treatments.

Can I get it with colour in the same visit?

Yes — it's a popular combination. We'll sequence the services correctly to protect both results.
